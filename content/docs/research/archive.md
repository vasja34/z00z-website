## Page Brief

What
: Historical notes, external article reviews, exploratory drafts, and archived research.

When
: Used for provenance, not as primary public truth.

Where
: Research footer, not primary navigation.

Who
: Researchers and maintainers looking for background context.

Why
: Archive material may be useful, but active pages should not inherit stale or unverified claims.

How
: Mark archived status, add warning banners, link to superseding active docs, and keep search filtering explicit.

## Section Lens

Source
: whitepapers, technical papers, done papers, benchmark notes, verification plans, and archive material.

Message
: research pages should preserve source depth while separating active authority from historical context.

UX
: a document index with filters, abstracts, status labels, and deep links into exact source files.

Include
: status tags, reading order, source links, superseded notices, benchmark caveats, and open questions.

Avoid
: treating archive drafts as canonical or flattening all papers into an unprioritized list.
